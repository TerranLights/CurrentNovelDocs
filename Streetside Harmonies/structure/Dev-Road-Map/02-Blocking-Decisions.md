# Blocking Decisions

Decisions that must be resolved before substantive work can proceed in the areas they affect. None of these are questions with an obvious right answer — they are choices that have been left open, and leaving them open would mean building downstream work on undefined ground.

**What qualifies as a blocking decision (not just an open question):** something a later phase genuinely cannot proceed past without an answer. Typical sources for a novel:
- An ambiguous chapter split or merge in a hand-written outline (which chapter does this stanza actually belong to?)
- A character *identity* question — is this a new character, or an existing one appearing under a different label? (This is different from a character simply not having a proper name assigned yet — see the note below.)
- A structural choice with real downstream consequences (POV, tense, whether a subplot is kept or cut)

**What does NOT qualify as a blocking decision:**
- **A character not yet having a proper name.** Personality, arc, and role can be fully designed under a placeholder/shorthand identifier — naming is settled whenever convenient and never gates character or plot work.
- **Theme not yet being clear.** Theme is discovered from finished or partially-finished plot/character/structure work, not designed in advance — see `07-Phase-5-Theme.md`. It is never logged here as blocking.
