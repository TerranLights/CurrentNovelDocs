# Streetside Harmonies — Development Roadmap

## What This Is

A structured development roadmap for *Streetside Harmonies*, a standalone verse novel about a street-musician trio. This roadmap covers **planning and outline work only** — it is not a drafting schedule or a word-count tracker. Every item here is a decision, a piece of structural design, or a completion status, not prose.

---

## Guiding Principles for This Roadmap

### Order follows dependency

The real-world writing order runs: **who are these people, and what is this place? → what do they do? → what does it all mean?** In roadmap terms: **Character (and Setting) creates Plot; Plot creates Theme.**

1. Setting (Phase 1) and character identity/personality/arc (Phase 2) come first.
2. Supporting cast/groups (Phase 3) — decided at the same time as character work.
3. Plot (Phase 4) — what do these people, with these wants, actually do?
4. Theme (Phase 5) — what does it all mean? Discovered, not designed upfront.

### The universal north star

Every novel in this shared universe answers some version of the same two governing questions: **what is the Robot Experience (the counterpart to the Human Experience), and how do the Robot Experience and the Human Experience interact and relate?** This pair is fixed across the whole line.

**This novel's own north star:** *What does it mean to have a home?* — Streetside Harmonies' own concrete derivation of the universal pair, worked out through Valerie (a robot with no legal claim to any home), Craig (a human who's never had a stable one), and Martin (whose drive for professional validation has cost him one).

### Systems before content
A character arc that references a plot beat that isn't settled is an arc that will need rewriting. Settle the foundations before writing the content that depends on them.

### Consult the shared framework, don't reinvent it
This novel's beat structure and character-arc format come from `reference/story-structure-definitions.md`, shared across all novels in this line.
