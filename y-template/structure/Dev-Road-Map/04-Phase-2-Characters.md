# Phase 2 — Characters

**Prerequisite:** Phase 1 complete.

**Runs alongside:** Phase 3 (Supporting Cast) — group/organization "wants" are decided at the same time as character work, not as a separate later step. Also parallel with Phase 6 (World Depth).

**What this phase does:** Establishes who these people are — protagonist(s), companions, and secondary cast — to the point where the actual chapter-by-chapter writing in Phase 4 can proceed with confidence. This is "who are these people, and what is this place?" — the first thing settled, before plot (Phase 4) and long before theme (Phase 5).

**A character does not need a proper name to be complete here.** Personality, arc, and role can be fully designed under a placeholder/shorthand identifier (a role, a working nickname, a bracketed description). Assign the actual proper name whenever it's convenient, per the naming conventions settled in Phase 1 — it is never a prerequisite for the rest of this phase.

---

## Character Design Standard

A character is complete when a writer who has never met this character can:
- Write any scene they appear in with confidence
- Write their reaction to any plot event
- Write their dialogue in any register (light, emotional, confrontational, curious)
- Know what they would never say or do

This requires: a personality description (not traits, but how they think and what they're organized around), a voice (how they speak, what their sentence rhythms are, what they avoid), a set of core convictions and at least one blind spot, a history that explains why they are the way they are, and a role in the story that means something beyond moving the plot along.

---

## 2.1 — Core Cast: Full Arc Completion

For the protagonist (`structure/character arcs/doll.txt`) and each companion (`huMan-1.txt`, `huMan-2.txt`):
- Background bio complete (`background notes/characters/`)
- Full arc complete against the fused beat structure in `story-structure-definitions.md` — every beat filled in, not left as a blank header
- Enneagram type (or equivalent psychological framework) and its stress/integration directions identified, if this novel uses one (see the Benson Trio novel's character files for a worked example)

## 2.2 — Secondary Characters

For every named (or not-yet-named) character beyond the core cast who appears in more than a walk-on capacity:
- Enough of the Character Design Standard above to write them confidently in their scenes
- Their relationship to the thematic throughline is *not* checked here — theme comes later (Phase 5)

## 2.3 — Minor/Walk-On Characters

Characters who appear briefly (a single scene, a single line) don't need full arcs, but should have enough to keep them consistent if they recur — a one-line personality note is often sufficient.
