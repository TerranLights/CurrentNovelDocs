# Valerie/Craig interruption conflicts — working list

**The six criteria (unchanged):** (1) Valerie makes plans and involves Craig unsolicited; (2) she genuinely means well; (3) her plans are fully in-character for her; (4) they interrupt something Craig was already doing; (5) Craig's interrupted plans are fully in-character for him; (6) the two sets of objectives come into real conflict.

**Design notes for this list, as distinct from the earlier Grok batches:**
- Grounded in the actual world-building (siligel, battery/charge, flight mode, tariffs/no passports for robots, a human mechanic who repairs her with physical tools) — no invented gadgets (no photonic emitters, nanites, replicators).
- Stakes vary on purpose — most of these are recoverable/annoying, a few are genuinely costly. Not every entry ends in permanent destruction; that flattened Valerie into a menace rather than someone lovably chaotic.
- Several are built directly around the want/need throughline: her external want (to travel, to experience everywhere) vs. her internal need (to be present in one thing) — using the already-established detail that she hoards Cascadian/Midwest/Colorado dollars and Sonoran pesos "to stay hopeful about travelling, even knowing that she can't."
- Craig's interrupted activities are pulled from his actual spec sheet: vinyl collecting, the mini Zen garden, the bonsai, his coffee ritual, painting, and his stated goal of getting solid content together for the demo/tape audition.

---

### 1. The Bus That Isn't
**Craig's plan:** He finally has two clear hours before Martin's back, and he's unstuck at last on the lyrics he needs for the tape audition.
**Valerie's plan:** She's heard about a discount charter to the Sonoran border leaving that afternoon, has already counted out her hoarded pesos, and packed a bag for both of them — certain that going *somewhere, anywhere* is exactly what will shake him loose, because it's what would shake her loose.
**Conflict:** They actually make it to the checkpoint before hitting the wall the story has already established — no passport, robots charged as cargo, turned away. Nothing invented; the world's own rules do the damage. This is the most direct dramatization of her want (escape) crashing into what the story will actually allow her (nothing).

### 2. Second-Hand Coup
**Craig's plan:** Weeks of saving for one shot at a rare pre-war pressing before another collector gets to it first.
**Valerie's plan:** Busking gossip about a "way better" flea market two towns over, a ride leaving in twenty minutes, certain comparison-shopping is the smarter move.
**Conflict:** While they're gone chasing the better option, someone else buys the record. Real, earned stakes from an established hobby — no gadget required.

### 3. The Impromptu Interview
**Craig's plan:** Quietly rehearsing what he'll say to the tape-audition scout, coffee ritual and all.
**Valerie's plan:** She overhears a touring correspondent doing man-on-the-street pieces on eccentric acts and hauls Craig over to be interviewed on the spot — she genuinely believes the exposure helps the band, which lines up with her own Save-the-Cat plan of chasing the record deal.
**Conflict:** His need for routine and preparation gets steamrolled by forced spontaneity in front of a stranger.

### 4. The Notice Nobody Signed For
**Craig's plan:** Once the land-acquisition threat looms, he's carefully drafting a polite, procedural letter to the developer's office — his coping style is "be correct, be patient, fix this properly."
**Valerie's plan:** She decides the real fix is busking outside the developer's office that same afternoon to charm him with music, gear packed before Craig's even finished his draft.
**Conflict:** The actual philosophical fault line in their relationship — procedure vs. charisma — playing out on the plot thread that matters most.

### 5. Bonsai Weather Forecast
**Craig's plan:** A months-long precise watering/pruning schedule for the bonsai.
**Valerie's plan:** A card flip convinces her "change is coming," and the tree needs to feel the real sky today — moved outside before he can stop her.
**Conflict:** Nothing ruined forever, just a missed watering window and a mildly stressed plant. Low register — this is where most of her interruptions should live.

### 6. The Lava-Lamp Verdict
**Craig's plan:** Twenty quiet minutes raking the mini Zen garden — it's what lets him sit down calm enough to compose afterward.
**Valerie's plan:** The lava-lamp wall "picks" the abandoned fairground carousel across town as today's outing; she's got both their shoes by the door before he's finished.
**Conflict:** He loses the headspace, not the garden — a time-and-mood cost rather than a destroyed object.

### 7. The Wrong Kind of Practice
**Craig's plan:** A rare quiet stretch (Martin's out with Rebecca) to nail down a hard time-signature part on the Hang for a specific song.
**Valerie's plan:** Having just watched the Jazz cats jam effortlessly, she decides Craig needs "real" spontaneous energy and invites them over unannounced for an impromptu session to loosen him up.
**Conflict:** He's not ready to play in front of anyone yet — this hits his Core Fear (no personal significance of his own) directly, turning her gift into an anxiety spiral instead of the loosening-up she intended.

### 8. The Currency She Can't Spend
**Craig's plan:** The unglamorous, responsible task of budgeting their thin busking money toward actual studio time for the demo.
**Valerie's plan:** In a burst of "we deserve something good today," she's already spent some of the shared savings on an exotic find from a traveling vendor.
**Conflict:** Throws off the budget and forces an uncomfortable money conversation right when he's protecting their one real shot at studio time — her impulse-spending and her hoarded-currency travel fantasy are the same impulse pointed in different directions.

### 9. Directions via Lava Lamp, Destination via Debt
**Craig's plan:** When the record-label exec first surfaces, he wants to check the company's credentials and read the fine print before responding to anything.
**Valerie's plan:** A card pull already told her it "felt good," so she's told the exec's office they'll take the meeting *today*, no time left for Craig to check anything.
**Conflict:** He walks into the meeting unprepared and anxious — and since we know this exec turns out to be the Fake Ally, her optimism here is dramatically ironic in a way Craig's caution would have caught.

### 10. The Painting Interrupted by a Sign
**Craig's plan:** A rare flow-state painting session after a long dry spell — exactly the kind of follow-through he struggles with.
**Valerie's plan:** She's decided a stray cat she's been chasing around the neighborhood needs rescuing *right now* and needs his help catching it.
**Conflict:** By the time the cat's caught, the light and the mood are gone and he can't get back into the painting — an emotional/creative cost rather than a material one, and a direct hit on his stated flaw ("difficulty seeing things through to fruition").

### 11. A Home for a Day
**Craig's plan:** During the abandoned-building period, a quiet nesting ritual — arranging what little they have to make the space feel livable, keeping morale up.
**Valerie's plan:** Believing routine is the enemy of their spirits right now, she spontaneously turns the space into a "living art installation" for the night — rearranging everything, painting slogans on the walls — meant as a morale-boosting surprise.
**Conflict:** This is the highest-register emotional one on the list: "home" is exactly what Craig is grieving, and her surprise undoes the one stabilizing thing he had control over. Good candidate for a scene during BGCI.

### 12. Busking Setlist Hijack
**Craig's plan:** A deliberately sequenced setlist for the day, built to slowly grow a crowd toward a big finish — a rare moment of him taking charge of the band's public performance.
**Valerie's plan:** Sensing the crowd's getting "too predictable," she calls an audible mid-performance and launches into a different, unplanned song.
**Conflict:** Public and real-time — unlike the private ones on this list, this happens in front of a crowd, raising the stakes of "everyone can see this go sideways."

### 13. The Souvenir from Nowhere
**Craig's plan:** Quietly re-cataloguing his vinyl collection by year — a soothing, structured task.
**Valerie's plan:** Coming back from a walk with some random object she's decided is a "souvenir" from an imaginary trip she's pretending they took, insisting on displaying it prominently in the middle of his organized shelves.
**Conflict:** Low-stakes and a little funny — he has to explain a broken ceramic gnome sitting in his 1930s jazz section. Small-scale version of the same travel-longing that drives entry #1.

### 14. The Reconciliation Ambush
**Craig's plan:** Quietly processing his feelings about his estranged mother, Sandra, privately in his journal — not acting, just sitting with it.
**Valerie's plan:** She believes wholeheartedly in the story's own stated theme ("through the power of love, family, and courage, they can overcome the odds together"), so she's already tracked down Sandra's contact and arranged a meeting, certain that reconciling will finally make Craig happy.
**Conflict:** The highest emotional stakes on this list. Nothing physical is broken, but she doesn't grasp how deep the wound actually goes — this hits his psychological need (peace with reality) at its rawest point, and the fallout is real, not slapstick. Good candidate for a scene that actually changes how Craig sees her for a while.

### 15. The Schimba Diversion
**Craig's plan:** Scraping together enough from busking to get the sleeve of his one prized pre-war record professionally restored — a narrow appointment window at a shop that doesn't rebook easily.
**Valerie's plan:** She quietly diverts some of the shared cash at a street currency exchange (a schimba) into Sonoran pesos, the same hopeless-hopeful travel gesture already established in her background notes — genuinely thinking it's smart to always have some ready to go.
**Conflict:** There's not enough left for the restoration slot that day, and it can't be rebooked soon. Small, grounded, scarcity-driven stakes — no gadgets, just a tight budget.

### 16. The Captcha Hour
**Craig's plan:** A rare, carefully arranged night in for just the two of them — his version of a home he can love, in miniature.
**Valerie's plan:** Before the date can start, she pulls him into her established weird-luck ritual of "playing Captcha" for good fortune, and it stretches into an hour neither of them meant to spend.
**Conflict:** Low-stakes and genuinely sweet — nothing's ruined, just the mood and timing of the evening get thrown, a good register-check entry showing her specific established quirks in action rather than a new invention.

### 17. Ricardo's Emergency Appointment
**Craig's plan:** Rare one-on-one rehearsal time with Martin on a complicated arrangement, scheduled for weeks.
**Valerie's plan:** She's noticed a small flicker in her own diagnostics and, rather than say anything directly, decides on her own to see Ricardo immediately and asks Craig to tag along "just in case" — pulling him out of rehearsal without warning.
**Conflict:** This one isn't chaos-for-fun — it's driven by her actual wound (can't ask for help directly, "it's not okay to depend on anyone for anything"). The unsolicited plan here reveals vulnerability rather than whimsy, which is worth having at least one of on this list.

### 18. Double-Booked Shopping Trip
**Craig's plan:** A rare solo visit to a specific antique shop he's been saving up a visit for.
**Valerie's plan:** She's already arranged for both of them to hit her favorite 2nd-hand exotic-clothing stalls that same afternoon, assuming he'd love to combine trips since "it's basically the same kind of thing."
**Conflict:** Mild and comedic — no quiet solo browsing, and by the time they get to his shop, it's closed. Time-cost only.

### 19. Teaching Out of Turn
**Craig's plan:** Patiently, quietly teaching Martin something in his own deliberate way — a rare bid for a real bonding moment with his brother.
**Valerie's plan:** True to her half of the story's "circle of teaching" (she's the one who teaches Martin the value of randomness), she interrupts to make the lesson "more fun" with an improv game.
**Conflict:** Subtle and values-based rather than material — her instinct isn't wrong for *her* arc, but it steps on the specific moment Craig wanted with Martin. Good for showing the circle-of-teaching dynamic creating real friction, not just eventual harmony.

### 20. The Fairgrounds Preview
**Craig's plan:** One quiet day scouting exactly where they'll busk during the upcoming county fair — careful, practical logistics.
**Valerie's plan:** She's decided they should "test the vibe" today by going in improvised disguises and treating it like a spy mission instead.
**Conflict:** Mild and comedic, but doubles as foreshadowing texture ahead of the fair without touching the actual Tesla-coil beat already planned there — the real scouting has to happen all over again another day.

### 21. The Quiet at Alan's Grave
**Craig's plan:** A private, unhurried moment alone at his father's grave in Monterey — the actual reason for the whole hitchhike.
**Valerie's plan:** Wanting to "make it special" rather than heavy, she's arranged a small musical tribute at the graveside and pulls Martin in to join without asking Craig first.
**Conflict:** Denies Craig the solitary grief ritual he needed, turning it into a group moment he didn't consent to — a vulnerable, plot-critical setting. Note: this sits right alongside the already-planned B-Story beat here (Valerie telling Martin he used to sound better), so it should be written as a companion beat within that same scene, not a replacement for it.

### 22. Rationing Day
**Craig's plan:** Carefully managing the day's siligel ration on the road, worked out to the gram to get Valerie to the next town.
**Valerie's plan:** Freshly inspired after Iman's story about the Vegas Tesla-Coil-Orchestra success, she burns extra charge running an impromptu "test performance" of new ideas roadside.
**Conflict:** Real physical stakes tied to an already-established mechanic (siligel scarcity, the canon detail that they eventually run out and have to carry her) — no invention needed, just her optimism spending down a resource Craig was protecting.

### 23. The Ruins of San Jose
**Craig's plan:** Wants to walk the San Jose war ruins alone for a while to sketch some of the wreckage — his passion for beauty found even in decay.
**Valerie's plan:** She's turned the ruins into a scavenger-hunt game and wants him in on it immediately, worried about him wandering off alone somewhere unstable.
**Conflict:** His need for solitary creative processing against her protective-but-controlling impulse — adds texture to the already-noted "ruins of war" leg of the trip.

### 24. Busking for Bus Fare
**Craig's plan:** A short, precisely curated busking set in a town square, timed to earn just enough for the next leg's transportation.
**Valerie's plan:** Riding the momentum of a growing crowd, she extends the set with unplanned material — better tips, but it runs long.
**Conflict:** Her plan isn't wrong exactly — they make more money — but they miss the day's last ride out of town because of it. A good example of her intentions succeeding on their own terms while still colliding with his actual plan.

### 25. The Barter That Wasn't Needed
**Craig's plan:** Carefully trading their few scavenged goods for practical necessities — food, siligel, tools — to get through the week in the abandoned building.
**Valerie's plan:** She trades away some of the group's meager goods to a passerby for a "lucky" trinket or a small piece of decoration for their squat, believing it'll lift morale when they need it most.
**Conflict:** Genuine scarcity cost at their lowest point — less to trade for necessities that week. No invention required, just real stakes.

### 26. The Overheard Gig
**Craig's plan:** Keeping a deliberately low profile — staying safe and unnoticed in a semi-legal squat.
**Valerie's plan:** She overhears talk of a "found-space performance" trend and wants to advertise an impromptu show right at the building to help the band get noticed.
**Conflict:** The highest-jeopardy entry on the list — risks exposing their squatting situation to a landlord, the police, or the developer himself, at the exact moment they can least afford that risk.

## Proposed shortlist (4 / 3 / 2)

A recommendation, not a final call — picked for the strongest tie to plot-critical beats and the widest emotional range, rather than pure comedic texture:

**Act 1 (4):**
- **#4 The Notice Nobody Signed For** — sits right against the actual catalyst (the developer's threat)
- **#14 The Reconciliation Ambush** — the deepest character wound on the list; only works while there's still a home to invite Sandra to
- **#1 The Bus That Isn't** — plants the want/need theme early so it resonates through the rest of the book
- **#9 Directions via Lava Lamp, Destination via Debt** — introduces the record exec with built-in dramatic irony (we already know he's the Fake Ally)

**Road of Trials (3):**
- **#21 The Quiet at Alan's Grave** — the single most emotionally loaded location in this stretch
- **#22 Rationing Day** — ties straight into the established siligel-scarcity mechanic
- **#24 Busking for Bus Fare** — distinctly "on the road" stakes, and her plan succeeding on its own terms while still costing him something

**Bad Guys Close In (2):**
- **#11 A Home for a Day** — the emotional standout for this stretch (undoing the one stabilizing thing he has)
- **#26 The Overheard Gig** — highest real-world jeopardy, tied straight to the central threat (eviction/exposure)

The rest (#2, 3, 5–8, 10, 12, 13, 15–20, 23, 25) stay in the file as backlog if any of these get cut or you want alternates.
