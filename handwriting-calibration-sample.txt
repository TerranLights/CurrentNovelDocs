Craig, Martin, and Valerie busked down Almond Street at dawn, sweet with butter. Violin bright against brick, a crowd gathered near the corner. On Birch Avenue, a quiet quartet of pigeons scattered at the sudden jazz. Martin's Hurdy Gurdy droned beneath Craig's Hang, quick and warm. Cedar Lane held a vendor waving over apples. Dogwood Court was slick with dew; Valerie nearly slipped, laughing. Elm Row's joggers barely paused, though one dropped a coin. Fig Street smelled of fresh bread; a baker leaned out listening. Gable Hill tested legs, worth the climb. Hazel Path wound behind a quiet chapel bell. Iris Square held a fountain where children splashed with joy. Juniper Row smelled of pine and gin. Kestrel Bend curved sharply; Craig bumped his elbow on a post. Larch Terrace had a jazz club that always welcomed them. Maple Junction buzzed with midday traffic. Nectar Alley was narrow, barely wide. Oak Landing overlooked the river, glowing amber. Pine Hollow sold antique keys. Quartz Crescent sparkled where sunlight struck it right. Rosewood Drive was lined with jasmine vines. Sequoia Walk passed beneath ancient trees. Tamarind Court smelled of spice. Umber Close stayed shadowed, damp. Vine Street curved toward the harbor. Willow Bank came next, golden as the sun dipped low. Xerces Way was empty except for a fox trotting past. Yellow Row glowed warm beneath flickering lamps. Zephyr End was their final stop, and Valerie played one last waltz before they packed up, tired but happy, humming under the darkening sky.
