# The Saga of Maggie Aarden — Development Roadmap (Series Level)

## What This Is

A structured development roadmap for *The Saga of Maggie Aarden* as a whole series. This tracks series-wide decisions and status — Maggie's own throughline, the shared setting, and how the books relate to each other. **Series structure corrected 2026-07-08: this is a 4-book series** — organized primarily around Maggie's own archetypal progression (drawing on K.M. Weiland's six-stage *Writing Archetypal Character Arcs* framework), not the 2-book/one-per-boyfriend structure originally assumed. Each book will likely ship as 2 physical print volumes (1,000+ pages each) purely for binding practicality — that split has no narrative meaning. See `02-Blocking-Decisions.md`, Decision 1, for full detail — the exact archetype-to-book mapping is still undetermined, so the `Book 1/` / `Book 2/` folders currently in this project are provisional and will need restructuring. **Each book will have its own Dev-Road-Map** once the real structure is settled, per `06-Phase-4-Story-and-Chapters.md`'s guidance on standalone-vs-series scale.

This roadmap covers **planning and outline work only** — not prose, not a drafting schedule.

## How to Read This

Same seven-phase structure as every other novel in this line (see `03` through `09`, plus `10-Craft-Reference.md`). At the series level, Phase 2 (Characters) covers Maggie herself — the one constant across the whole saga. Each book's own Phase 2 covers that book's human companion.

## Current State

*As of 2026-07-08 — this series exists as a premise/cast sketch given verbally by the author; no chapters, no book count confirmed beyond the two known human-companion eras, no full outline for either book.*

**Overall completion: ~10%** — Maggie's throughline and the series' historical endpoint (the Falkland Treaty) are established; almost everything else, including the actual book count, is open.

What is solid:

Each stage is expected ("very likely") to run as a shadow variant (active and/or passive) rather than a clean Positive Change Arc, reflecting that Maggie is pushed through each stage against her will. ## Guiding Principles for This Roadmap

### Order follows dependency

The real-world writing order runs: **who are these people, and what is this place? → what do they do? → what does it all mean?** In roadmap terms: **Character (and Setting) creates Plot; Plot creates Theme.**

### The universal north star

Every novel in this shared universe answers some version of the same two governing questions: **what is the Robot Experience (the counterpart to the Human Experience), and how do the Robot Experience and the Human Experience interact and relate?** This pair is fixed across the whole line.

**This series' own north star:** *[[—]]* — not yet identified. Given that this series ends at the exact historical hinge point where robots lose the personhood/rights they'll spend centuries trying to regain, a strong candidate thread is something like *"what does it cost to become a symbol instead of a person?"* — Maggie never wanted leadership, and the story's core irony is that her desire to be left alone is precisely what history won't allow. Not locked in — per Phase 5, this should be confirmed once more plot work exists.

### Consult the shared framework, don't reinvent it
This series' beat structure and character-arc format come from `reference/story-structure-definitions.md`, shared across all novels in this line. This series also has an unusually direct relationship to the shared-universe timeline itself — treat `reference/story-structure-definitions.md` and the universe-timeline reference as required reading, not optional background.
