Maggie Aarden starts out as a simple, humble robot working industrial yards — machinery, scrap, hardware, and vehicles. As anti-robot sentiment rises among humans, Maggie is unwillingly thrust into a position of quiet, pre-war leadership over her fellow robots — organizing a "third option" resettlement into separate, parallel communities, neither fighting nor submitting. **She does not cause the War of Upper Earth** — the war breaks out for its own separate reasons — but it escalates her into a position of full leadership over her people that she never wanted. This culminates in Upper Earth's own governments "escorting" her via South America to the Falkland Islands and "inviting" her to sign the Falkland Treaty — having already settled on her as their choice of signatory, using her established credibility as a robot leader to make the exile look consented-to rather than simply imposed — thus forcing all robots across the world to "willingly self-exile" to their new homeland of Antarctica.

Across the saga, Maggie has two human companions — never at the same time, and the two never meet each other. Her first boyfriend is initially shy and quiet, and gains a sense of confidence in himself over the course of their time together. Her second boyfriend (later husband) is initially proud, boisterous, and brash, and eventually gains a sense of humility, coming to fight alongside Maggie for a cause greater than himself.

**Major shared-universe significance:** Maggie appears to be the actual historical figure behind the real Falkland Treaty signing (June 21, 2564) referenced throughout the InnerTepeniaGDD's own world history — which currently lists "who signed it and on whose authority" as an open TBD.
