A robot musician travels across four realms — King, Warrior, Magician, and Lover, named after Robert Moore & Douglas Gillette's "King, Warrior, Magician, Lover" masculine-archetype framework — retrieving an artifact from each and, ultimately, uniting them (mechanism TBD).

Originally conceived (2019) as a story spanning "four realities": King (land-based civilization), Lover (seafaring, communicates entirely through symbol and song, no spoken language), Warrior (a technological, mathematical version of ancient Sparta), and Magician ("basically in the wild"). The author's original notes imposed a Father/Mother, physical/metaphysical split on these four (King+Lover=physical/Father, Warrior+Magician=metaphysical-ethereal/Mother) — **confirmed 2026-07-08 via research that this split does not exist in Moore & Gillette's actual book**, meaning it was the author's own invention and is fully theirs to redesign. Current direction (2026-07-08): exactly one of the four realms is ethereal/metaphysical, reinterpreted as some kind of simulation in sci-fi terms — either Lover or Magician, not yet decided. See `structure/things to introduce.md` and the standalone file `four-realm musician - architecture options (Lover vs Magician).md` (at the CurrentNovelDocs repo root, outside this project folder) for the full arguments on both sides.

**Origin material:**
- A 2019 dream in which two girls give the author a burned CD-R full of new music; he's thrilled, and wakes up before he can bring it home — the felt impossibility of carrying something out of one reality into another is the thematic seed for the whole artifact-retrieval structure.
- "Seaside Chorus" (poem, 2013) — a solitary guitarist playing wordless, nylon-string music alone on a moonlit beach at night, joined by a mysterious "ghostly note." This is the poem the musician-hero was originally written about, and gives him his core sensibility: playing for no one, yet somehow part of something greater.

**Tepenian universe integration (decided 2026-07-08):** this series is being adapted into the shared Tepenian sci-fi universe. The musician is a robot/doll — not a separate human character alongside a doll protagonist, but one and the same. Full integration details (setting placement on the shared timeline, Earth vs. off-world, how the four realms/civilizations map onto established or undeveloped lore) are still open — see `structure/Dev-Road-Map/00-Overview.md`.
