# The Four-Realm Musician (title TBD) — Development Roadmap (Series Level)

## What This Is

A structured development roadmap for this four-volume series. Tracks series-wide decisions and status — the Musician's own throughline, the shared four-realm structure, and how the books relate to each other. **Each book also has its own Dev-Road-Map** (`Book - King/`, `Book - Warrior/`, `Book - Magician/`, `Book - Lover/`, each under `structure/Dev-Road-Map/`).

This roadmap covers **planning and outline work only** — not prose, not a drafting schedule.

---

## Current State

*As of 2026-07-08 — this series exists as a reconstructed premise, originally conceived in 2019 and never written down in full (the author kept the details in memory only). Being actively rebuilt through conversation. No chapters or full outline exist yet for any of the four books.*

**Overall completion: ~5%** — the core structural concept (four realms, one artifact each, unite them) and the protagonist's essential character are established; almost everything else, including which realm is the series' one ethereal/simulated realm, is open.

What is solid:
- Series structure: four volumes, one per realm (King, Warrior, Magician, Lover), each centered on retrieving that realm's artifact
- The Musician: a robot/doll (per the 2026-07-08 decision to adapt this into the Tepenian universe), plays wordless nylon-string music, originally written about in the poem "Seaside Chorus" (`reference/reference articles/Seaside Chorus (poem).md`)
- Confirmed via research: the King/Warrior/Magician/Lover archetype names come from Robert Moore & Douglas Gillette's real book, but that book has **no** Father/Mother or physical/metaphysical split — that structure was the author's own invention, meaning it's freely open to redesign rather than something to reconcile against a source
- Current direction: exactly one of the four realms is ethereal/simulated, the other three are physically real — narrowed to a choice between Lover and Magician, not yet decided (see `02-Blocking-Decisions.md` and the standalone architecture-options file at the `CurrentNovelDocs` root)
- Thematic seed material: a 2019 dream about receiving a CD-R of new music and being unable to carry it out of the dream into waking reality — the likely emotional core of the whole "retrieve artifacts from other realms" premise

What is incomplete, in priority order:
1. Lover vs. Magician as the ethereal/simulated realm (see Blocking Decisions)
2. What sends the Musician on this journey in the first place — not yet identified
3. What the four specific artifacts actually are, and what happens when they're united
4. The Musician's own name
5. Placement within the Tepenian universe — Earth-side (which era?) or off-world/space
6. Any named characters within any of the four realms
7. Full beat-by-beat structure for any of the four books

## Guiding Principles for This Roadmap

### Order follows dependency

The real-world writing order runs: **who are these people, and what is this place? → what do they do? → what does it all mean?** In roadmap terms: **Character (and Setting) creates Plot; Plot creates Theme.**

### The universal north star

Every novel in this shared universe answers some version of the same two governing questions: **what is the Robot Experience (the counterpart to the Human Experience), and how do the Robot Experience and the Human Experience interact and relate?** This pair is fixed across the whole line.

**This series' own north star:** *[[—]]* — not yet identified, and given how early-stage this reconstruction is, that's expected. One candidate worth watching: the tension between the Musician's own lived knowledge (from the CD-R dream) that nothing can be carried out of an unreal space into reality, and his entire quest depending on doing exactly that.

### Consult the shared framework, don't reinvent it
This series' beat structure and character-arc format come from `reference/story-structure-definitions.md`, shared across all novels in this line.
