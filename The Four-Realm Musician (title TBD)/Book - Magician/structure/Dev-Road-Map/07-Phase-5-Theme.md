# Phase 5 — Theme

**Prerequisite: none, strictly speaking.** This phase is deliberately *not* gated the way the others are. Theme is discovered from plot, character, and structure work that's fully or partially built — it is not designed in advance and it is never a blocking decision (see `02-Blocking-Decisions.md`). In practice this phase tends to start somewhere during or after Phase 4, once there's enough plot and character on the page to actually look at, but there's no hard rule requiring Phase 4 to be finished first — work on this whenever it becomes apparent, and revisit it as often as it needs.

**What this phase does:** Names what the story turns out to actually be about. This is "what does it all mean?" — the last thing settled, built on who these people are (Phase 2–3) and what they do (Phase 4), never the other way around.

---

## 5.1 — This Novel's Specific North Star

Every novel in this shared universe answers some version of the same two governing questions: *what is the Robot Experience, and how do the Robot Experience and the Human Experience interact and relate?* (See `00-Overview.md`.) That pair is fixed across the whole line.

What's specific to this book is its own concrete derivation of that pair — a single, plain-language question this particular story turns out to be asking. (Benson Trio's is *"what does it mean to have a home?"*) Don't force this early; look at the plot and character work already on the page and ask what it's actually circling around.

## 5.2 — Theme Stated Moment

Per `story-structure-definitions.md`'s Theme Stated beat: a concrete, situational line or moment (not an abstract statement of theme) that Act 3 later turns out to have been about all along. Identify where this lands in the finished (or mostly-finished) chapter breakdown.

## 5.3 — Recurring Symbols/Motifs

Any image, object, or motif the story returns to. These are often easier to spot after a full draft or full outline exists than to plan in advance — check the finished material for what's already recurring before inventing something new.

---

## Phase 5 Output Checklist

- [ ] This novel's specific north star identified
- [ ] Theme Stated moment identified (event + line)
- [ ] Recurring symbols/motifs identified, if any

**This phase has no strict completion gate — revisit it any time during Phase 4, 6, or 7 as the story comes into focus.**
